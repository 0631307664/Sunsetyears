About Me

Hello, I’m Nish Ramphal, a woman on a mission to inspire and transform the lives of women over 50. Growing up as a brown woman in a third-world country, I’ve seen how patriarchy and limited worldviews can confine women to roles within the home, workplace, and church. But I believe there’s a greater purpose for us all.

That’s why I believe we, as older women, must step up to become the mentors younger women desperately need.

But how can we guide others effectively if we’re not actively growing ourselves? We need to educate ourselves, take care of our physical and mental health, and nurture our spiritual lives.

Growing up, I watched my parents struggle with poverty and limited resources, but they persevered. Now, in our 50s, it’s our time to shine. Let’s break free from societal constraints, heal from past wounds, and step boldly into the life God has planned for us.

Making Christmas Bright: Finding Joy in the Empty Nest

Christmas is almost here, and like so many of you, I’ve been thinking about how different it feels these days. The kids have grown up, moved out, and started their own families. For the first time, it’s just my husband and me this year—our little empty nest at Christmas.

I’ll admit, it’s bittersweet. I miss the hustle and bustle of a full house—the excited chatter, the smell of cookies baking, and the joy of watching the kids unwrap gifts. Some of you might know exactly how this feels. Maybe your children are celebrating Christmas with their in-laws or off on a holiday adventure. Life has changed, hasn’t it?

But instead of feeling sad or lonely, I’ve decided to embrace this new season. Sure, it’s different, but that doesn’t mean it can’t be just as special.

This year, we’re keeping it simple but still festive. Life’s expensive, and like many of you, we’re mindful of our spending. But here’s a little story that made me smile. My husband came home the other day with the cutest little surprise—tiny lights he found at Value Home for just R5 each!

He bought ten of them, and they’re absolutely magical. I’ve placed them in jars and vases, scattering them around the house. The way they light up each corner is so cozy and cheerful. It’s such a small, inexpensive touch, but it’s made a big difference to how the house feels.

As I sit here now, with these twinkling lights glowing softly around me, I feel so grateful. It’s a reminder that Christmas isn’t about how big the celebration is or how perfect everything looks. It’s about appreciating the moments we have, even the quiet ones.

So, to anyone out there who might be spending Christmas without the usual crowd, let’s not let the emptiness steal our joy. This is a chance to slow down, to reflect, and to create new traditions. Maybe it’s decorating in a way you’ve never done before, baking something new, or simply enjoying a cozy evening with your favorite holiday movie.

This year, I’m embracing the quiet, the creativity, and the love that’s still so present. And you know what? These moments—simple and heartfelt—are what Christmas is all about.

Let’s make it beautiful, in our own way.

My Vision

My heart is for women who have felt trapped, limited, or unseen—especially those navigating the later stages of life. Together, we can break free from societal and personal constraints, embrace our individuality, and step into the plans God has for us. It’s never too late to reinvent yourself, heal from past wounds, and grow closer to God.

Some topics we’ll explore include:

  • Personal growth and self-awareness
  • Physical and mental wellness
  • Faith and spiritual growth
  • Mentoring and empowering younger women
  • Overcoming adversity and finding purpose
  • Bible studies and devotionals
  • Personal anecdotes and lessons learned

Let’s embark on this transformative journey together.
