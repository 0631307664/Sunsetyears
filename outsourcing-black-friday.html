How I Outsmarted Black Friday (After Falling for It Too Many Times!)

Let me tell you a little story about me and Black Friday. We’ve had some... well, complicated moments. You know that feeling when you see a big shiny “50% OFF EVERYTHING!” sign and suddenly think, “This is my moment! I’m going to save SO MUCH MONEY!”? Yep, that was me—year after year, falling headfirst into the Black Friday trap.

One year, I was determined to snag this fancy blender I’d been eyeing for months. It could puree soup, crush ice, and probably start a smoothie business on the side. I got up at 5 AM, braved the chaos, and... it was sold out. Devastated but unwilling to leave empty-handed, I somehow convinced myself I needed an electric egg poacher and a set of candles that smelled like pine trees. Spoiler: I hate poached eggs and the candles made my house smell like a forest.

That’s when I realized: Black Friday wasn’t saving me money—it was making me spend on things I didn’t even want! But here’s the good news. I’ve learned some lessons, and now I’ve got a strategy that helps me shop smarter without falling for those shiny deals.

Lesson 1: Make a List and Stick to It (Seriously!)

Let me tell you why I was such an easy target for Black Friday: I never had a plan. I’d walk into a store with vague ideas like, “Maybe I need a new toaster?” and leave with a banana-shaped beanbag chair. True story. It was “on sale,” and I convinced myself it would be a quirky conversation starter. Spoiler: the only conversations it started were me tripping over it and yelling, “Why do I even have this?!”

Now, I write a list before I shop. If it’s not on the list, it doesn’t go in the cart. Need a vacuum? Write it down. Need a banana beanbag? Nope. Not today, my yellow friend.

Lesson 2: Check Before You Trust Those Discounts

Here’s the thing about those “50% OFF!” signs—they’re not always what they seem. Retailers are clever. They sometimes mark up prices just before the sale so that “half off” isn’t much of a deal at all. Oh, I’ve fallen for this trick too many times.

One year, I was so proud of myself for buying a winter coat at half price. I strutted out of that store feeling like a financial genius... until my friend told me the coat had been cheaper before Black Friday. Ouch.

Now, I do my homework. I check prices ahead of time and compare them across stores. A few minutes of research can save you from buying something that’s only pretending to be a bargain.

The Wisdom of Savvy Shoppers

Here’s the thing—there are some people who totally crush it on Black Friday. They show up with a plan, a budget, and laser focus. They don’t fall for distractions or gimmicks. And honestly? I admire them. To all the savvy shoppers out there: teach us your ways!

Shopping like that is empowering. It’s about taking control, not letting retailers manipulate you. As Vicki Robin says in Your Money or Your Life: “Money is something we choose to trade our life energy for.” Doesn’t that make you want to spend it wisely?

A Little Biblical Wisdom

This all reminds me of Proverbs 21:5: “The plans of the diligent lead surely to abundance, but everyone who is hasty comes only to poverty.” Black Friday isn’t the enemy—it’s how we approach it. With a little planning and patience, we can make it work for us instead of against us.

A Laugh and a Lesson

Looking back, my Black Friday flops make me laugh now. The egg poacher? Never used it. The banana beanbag? Gifted it to someone else (with no explanation). The fancy blender I missed out on? Turns out, my old blender works just fine.

These days, I approach Black Friday with intention. I plan ahead, stick to my list, and avoid the hype. No more falling for Mr. Black Friday’s tricks. And you know what? It feels pretty great to leave the sales with my wallet—and my dignity—intact.

So, tell me—what’s your funniest Black Friday purchase? I’d love to hear your stories in the comments! Let’s laugh together and remind ourselves that we’ve all been there.

And remember: Don’t let Black Friday fool you. Outsmart it instead.
